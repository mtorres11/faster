What is F.A.S.T.E.R.?

The F.A.S.T.E.R. scale helps you recognize where you are on your restoration journey. Each letter represents a stage of decline; the first (Restoration) is the ideal state. Review the pillars honestly to identify areas that need attention.

F — Forgetting Priorities

It is the beginning of spiritual decline. The person begins to neglect communion with God and eternal priorities. There is no open fall yet, but an internal shift: less prayer, less vigilance, more distraction and self-sufficiency.

A — Anxiety

An internal noise of worry and fear arises. Practical trust in God decreases and thoughts become disordered. Resentment, speculation, and emotional tension appear.

S — Speeding Up

It is the fleshly response to anxiety. Instead of stopping and depending on God, the person speeds up: overcommits, works compulsively, or fills their schedule to avoid facing what they feel. Doing replaces resting in God.

T — Ticked Off (Anger / Constant Irritation)

Accumulated frustration turns into anger. There are overreactions, pride, and defensiveness. The person becomes more critical, less patient, and more prone to conflict.

E — Exhaustion

After living in anxiety, speeding up, and anger, collapse comes. There is physical, emotional, and spiritual fatigue. Joy and clarity are lost. It is a state of extreme vulnerability.

R — Relapse

It is the return to previously abandoned sinful or addictive behaviors. The person gives in to desire, loses control, and experiences guilt and shame. It is the final result of the downward process if not interrupted earlier.

How to use: Go through the pillars in order. In each one, check the phrases that describe your situation, fill in “Most powerful behavior” and the questions. Use Back and Next, or Review My Pillars to go to the pillars overview. The 💪 button moves that phrase to “Most powerful behavior”. If you fill a pillar, all previous ones must have something too.
